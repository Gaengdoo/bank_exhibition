RELIC

A petition and letter of authorization for the establishment of Daehan Cheonil Bank

As the founding document of Daehan Cheonil Bank, the contents of the bank establishment petition, including the founding philosophy written on January 22, 1899 are shown on the right. The official seal of the Financial Bureau license under the auspices of Emperor Gojong, approved on January 30 of the same year, is stamped on the left. (State-designated record No. 11 / Seoul tangible cultural property No. 279)
RELIC

A petition for the establishment of Incheon, Busan and Mokpo branches of Daehan Cheonil Bank and associated permit

Within two months of its establishment, Daehan Cheonil Bank submitted a petition to open branches in Incheon, Busan and Mokpo to the Financial Bureau. At the time, the opening of the Busan and Mokpo branches was canceled by Japanese intervention, and the Incheon branch was opened as the first branch of Woori Bank. (State-designated record No. 11 / Seoul tangible cultural property No. 279)
RELIC

Daehan Cheonil Bank’s List of the Order

In March 1902, the Korean Empire Imperial Household appointed King Youngchin as a shareholder and invested a large amount of capital. At the same time, it appointed him as the second president of the bank and designated Daehan Cheonil Bank as the Imperial Bank. (State-designated record No. 11 / Seoul tangible cultural property No. 279)
RELIC

Literary magazine 「Cheonil」

In April 1949, after the liberation, a literary magazine titled Cheonil, which was named after Daehan Cheonil Bank, was published. It dealt with various subjects such as the economy, literature, and history, and approached Woori Bank's history, formerly written during the Japanese colonial period, from a new point of view after the liberation.
